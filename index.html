I am an Americanist, scholar, and teacher. Currently, I'm working as Postdoctoral Fellow at Collaborative Research Center 1199: Processes of Spatialization under the Global Condition. I am the author of The West and the Word (de Gruyter 2020) and co-founder of the European Network for Minor Mobilities in the Americas (ENMMA) that brings together European scholars in the fields of transnational, border, and mobility studies. My publications and talks cover a breadth of intersectional fields and topics, among them spatial imaginations, human and cultural geographies, naturalist and postmodern literature, as well as film, horror, and catastrophe studies.

My research interests include

  • Imaginations of spaces and places
  • Archipelagic im/mobilities
  • Game studies and gamification
  • Criminality and youth delinquency

I am currently pursuing a habilitation project that explores transoceanic dynamics of US imperial and literary discourses.
