I am a first-generation scholar with a background in American Studies. Currently, I am working as a Postdoctoral Researcher at Leipzig University’s Collaborative Research Center 1199: Processes of Spatialization under the Global Condition. I'm the author of The West and the Word (de Gruyter, 2020). My other publications and talks cover a range of intersectional topics, including spatial imaginations, cultural geography and critical cartography, postmodern literature, as well as film and horror studies. Currently, I am pursuing a habilitation project that explores the transoceanic dynamics of US imperial and literary discourses.

Research interests
  • Imaginations of space and place
  • Archipelagic and island studies
  • Critical cartography
  • Digital humanities
  • Game studies
  • Criminality and youth delinquency
