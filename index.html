I am a first-generation scholar and teacher in the field of American Studies, working as a Postdoctoral Researcher at Collaborative Research Center 1199: Processes of Spatialization under the Global Condition. I am the author of The West and the Word (de Gruyter, 2020) and co-founder of the European Network for Minor Mobilities in the Americas (ENMMA) that brings together European scholars in the fields of transnational, border, and mobility studies. My publications and talks cover a breadth of intersectional fields and topics, among them spatial imaginations, human and cultural geographies, naturalist and postmodern literature, as well as film, horror, and catastrophe studies.

My research interests include
  • Imaginations of space and place
  • Archipelagic and island studies
  • Game and film studies
  • Criminality and delinquency

Currently, I am pursuing a habilitation project that explores transoceanic dynamics of US imperial and literary discourses.
