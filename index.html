I am a first-generation scholar with a background in American Studies, currently working at Leipzig University’s Collaborative Research Center 1199: Processes of Spatialization under the Global Condition. My publications and talks explore hidden connections and critical intersections of cultural discourses, including spatial imaginations, human geographies and critical cartography, postmodern literature, as well as film and horror studies. I'm the author of The West and the Word (de Gruyter, 2020). My habilitation project explores transoceanic dynamics in imperial and literary discourses of the United States.

Research Interests
  • Imaginations of space and place
  • Archipelagic and island studies
  • Critical cartography
  • Digital humanities
  • Game studies
  • Criminality and youth delinquency
