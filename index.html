I am an American Studies scholar, currently working as Postdoctoral Fellow at Leipzig University’s collaborative research center SFB (CRC) 1199 Processes of Spatialization under the Global Condition. I am the author of The West and the Word: Imagining, Formatting, and Ordering the American West in Nineteenth-Century Cultural Discourse (de Gruyter 2020) and co-founder of the European Network for Minor Mobilities in the Americas that brings together European scholars in the fields of transnational, border, and mobility studies. My various and intersectional topics, including spatial imaginations, border studies, naturalism, postmodernism, as well as film and horror studies.

My research interests include

  • Imaginations and representations of space
  • Mobility and border studies
  • Film, game, and horror studies
  • Criminality, survivalism, and dystopias
