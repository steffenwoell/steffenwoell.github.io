I am an American Studies scholar and teacher, currently working as Postdoctoral Fellow at collaborative research center CRC 1199: Processes of Spatialization under the Global Condition. I am the author of The West and the Word (de Gruyter 2020) and co-founder of the European Network for Minor Mobilities in the Americas that brings together European scholars in the fields of transnational, border, and mobility studies. My publications and talks cover a variety of intersectional fields and topics, among them spatial imaginations, human and cultural geographies, naturalist and postmodern literature, as well as film, horror, and catastrophe studies.

My research interests include

  • Imaginations / representations of space and place
  • Archipelagic im/mobilities
  • Game studies and gamification
  • Criminality and youth delinquency

I am currently pursuing a habilitation project that explores transoceanic dynamics of US imperial and literary discourses.
