I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at Leipzig University’s collaborative research center SFB (CRC) 1199 Processes of Spatialization under the Global Condition. I have published on various and intersectional topics in American Studies, including spatial imaginations, border studies, naturalism, postmodernism, as well as film and horror studies.

I am the author of The West and the Word: Imagining, Formatting, and Ordering the American West in Nineteenth-Century Cultural Discourse (de Gruyter 2020) and co-founder of the European Network for Minor Mobilities in the Americas that brings together European scholars in the fields of transnational, border, and mobility studies.

My research interests include, but are not limited to

    Cultural imaginations and representations of space

    Mobility and border studies

    Film, game, and horror studies

    Survivalism and (post)apocalypticism

Currently, I am pursuing a postdoctoral research project at SFB 1199 that investigates transoceanic dynamics of the United States’ imperial and literary discourses.
